Islamic Culture & Etiquette

Dress Code

Dress modestly, especially when visiting mosques or religious sites. Cover shoulders and knees. Women should bring a scarf for mosque visits. Beachwear is only for beaches/pools.

Left Hand Rule

Use your right hand for eating, giving/receiving items, and handshakes. The left hand is considered unclean in Islamic culture. Avoid pointing with your index finger.

Ramadan (Fasting Month)

During Ramadan, avoid eating/drinking in public during daylight hours out of respect. Many restaurants close during the day but open after sunset. Business hours may be shorter.

Prayer Times

Muslims pray 5 times daily. You'll hear the call to prayer (Adhan) from mosques. Be respectful during prayer times. Some businesses may close briefly for Friday prayers (Jumu'ah).

Alcohol & Pork

Indonesia is not a dry country, but alcohol is not widely available. Pork is prohibited in Islam and not commonly served. Look for "halal" labels on food products.

Mosque Visits

Remove shoes before entering mosques. Dress modestly (long pants, covered shoulders). Women may need to cover their head. Non-Muslims can usually visit outside prayer times.

Practical Travel Tips
🚕

Transportation

Use Gojek or Grab apps for safe, affordable rides. TransJakarta bus is efficient. Avoid regular taxis unless metered. Traffic is heavy, plan extra time.

💧

Water & Food

Don't drink tap water. Buy bottled water. Street food is generally safe but choose busy stalls. Wash hands frequently or carry hand sanitizer.

💰

Money Tips

Carry cash as many small vendors don't accept cards. ATMs widely available. Bargaining expected at markets. Keep small bills (50k, 20k IDR) handy.

📱

Internet & SIM

Get a local SIM card at airport or convenience stores (Telkomsel, XL, Indosat). 4G coverage is good. Free WiFi available in malls and cafes.

🏥

Health & Safety

Dengue fever risk - use mosquito repellent. Bring basic medicines. Major hospitals: RS Siloam, RSCM. Travel insurance recommended. Jakarta is generally safe.

💬

Language

Bahasa Indonesia is the official language. English spoken in tourist areas and hotels. Learn basic phrases: "Terima kasih" (Thank you), "Berapa?" (How much?).
